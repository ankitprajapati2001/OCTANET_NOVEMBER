About Dharavi

Dharavi is a slum located in the heart of Mumbai, India. It is one of the largest slums in Asia and one of the most densely populated places on Earth, with an estimated population of over 1 million people living in an area of just 2.16 square kilometers. Dharavi is a microcosm of India, with people from all over the country living and working together. It is a place of contrasts, where extreme poverty and wealth exist side by side. Dharavi is home to a wide range of industries, including leather tanning, recycling, pottery, and garment manufacturing. It is also a major center for the informal economy, with millions of people working in small businesses and workshops. Dharavi's industries contribute billions of dollars to the Indian economy each year.

Future of DHARAVI

The future of Dharavi is uncertain. The government's redevelopment plans have been met with resistance from residents, who fear that they will be displaced from their homes and businesses. However, the government has also promised to provide residents with housing and other assistance if redevelopment takes place.

It is important to note that Dharavi is not just a slum. It is a vibrant and dynamic community with a rich history and culture. The residents of Dharavi are hardworking and resourceful people who are making a significant contribution to the Indian economy. It is important to find a way to improve the lives of Dharavi's residents without displacing them from their homes.

Dharavi is a complex and fascinating place. It is a place of contrasts, where extreme poverty and wealth exist side by side. It is a place of challenges, but it is also a place of resilience and hope. The residents of Dharavi are determined to build a better future for themselves and their families.
